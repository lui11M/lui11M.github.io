Overview

Theme: The ridiculous prohibitions of the Estado Novo
My role: Creating and modelling the characters

The era of Estado Novo (New State) stands as a poignant testament to the intricacies of power and governance and their restrictions. Portugal bore witness to a regime characterised by authoritarianism and censorship, where gatherings of more than three people were deemed subversive acts, a facet that shaped the society and dynamics of socialising. This decree, aimed at stifling dissent and maintaining control, imposed a stark reality upon the populace.


In honour of the 50th birthday of the 25th of April, also known as the Carnation Revolution which put an end to the dictatorship regime in Portugal , my amazing team and I delve into the ridiculous forbidden rules of the Estado Novo (such as the prohibition gatherings of more that three people, kissing in public or even drinking Coca-Cola ) through the creation of a 3D animation, were our narratives will explore these prohibition, and the nations resistance and angst to this enforced limitations. This animation would then be videomapped in one of the oldest schools in Lisbon.

In parallel to this project, my group and I (which consisted of a collaboration between Global Design Bachelors and the Creative Tech Bachelors), had to have an interactive experience which contextualised the audience in regards to our project and had to create a brand/studio. Therefore we created Porque Sim Studios (in english it translates to Because Studios), our logo and brand identity can be found throughout our project.
